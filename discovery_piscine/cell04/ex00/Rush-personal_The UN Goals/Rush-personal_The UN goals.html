The UN Goals

What are they?
The UN goals are a set of seventeen goals set up in 2015, which are designed with the purpose of making our planet more sustainable, and to mitigate the impacts of climate change. The UN goals look at preventing hunger and poverty, growing the economy, creating a better world for the enviroment with clean and affordable energy, and a cleaner world for the creatures and plants on the planet, and trying to destroy inequality among the human race. The UN goals are the future.
The UN goals are a call to action, and there are a variety of ways people can help to achieve certain of the UN goals, and sign up to help make our planet more sustainable for the future generations. Currently, there are 3,804 events, and 7,406 actions going on to try and achieve the UN goals and make this planet more sustainable.

Why are they important?
The UN goals are the key to our future. They will assist future generations with growing up in a sustainable future, and continuing to evolve this planet into a better earth despite climate change and other in-sustainabily's.
